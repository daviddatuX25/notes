## Data Analysis

**Specific Objective 1 — Process Mapping and Workflow Analysis.** Semi-structured questionnaire responses and field observation notes from Guidance and Registrar staff are documented through process flow charts and analyzed through workflow analysis. The resulting process maps identify scoring bottlenecks, inter-office coordination failures, data integrity vulnerabilities, and notification deficiencies. These maps constitute the empirical foundation from which every NEXIAM feature is derived. No requirement enters development without a documented operational failure.

**Specific Objective 2 — Iterative Design Validation.** Completed modules are demonstrated to Guidance and Registrar staff during the construction cycle. Misalignments between system design and field-confirmed needs are logged as revision items, connecting every design change to a specific field finding. The functional requirements and system features tables simultaneously constitute the Chapter 3 system design section and the reference structure against which Cutover testing scenarios are built. User Design feedback, including mockup preferences and feature acceptance, is captured iteratively and documented within this objective.

**Specific Objective 3 — Development Documentation.** The RAD construction process is documented through four phases. Requirements Planning captures the operational baseline. User Design produces the RBAC schema and authorization matrix. Rapid Construction integrates the system components under human code review, with AI-assisted development accelerating implementation where appropriate. Cutover deploys the platform for evaluation. The development output includes the integrated NEXIAM platform, component integration decisions, and architectural trade-offs encountered during construction.

**Specific Objective 4 — Descriptive TAM Means.** Each respondent's items per construct are averaged into a single per-person construct score. These per-person scores are then averaged across all evaluator post-test responses to produce the pooled mean for Perceived Usefulness and Perceived Ease of Use. Means above the midpoint of the five-point scale (greater than 3.0) indicate positive acceptance. Standard deviations per construct are reported alongside the means to describe response consistency. Pooled means are reported with a breakdown of per-role descriptive means for each construct: Registrar Personnel, Guidance Personnel, First-Year Students, and Program Heads. Results are interpreted against established TAM benchmarks from the original Davis (1989) instrument and subsequent validation literature.
